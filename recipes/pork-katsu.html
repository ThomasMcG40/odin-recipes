This pork katsu has been breaded and fried to perfection! Try it yourself!

This signature Japanese dish uses panko bread crumbs to deliver the perfect crunch in every bite, alongside a tasty tonkatsu sauce to give you a food experience you will never forget.

If you are a lover of Japanese food, this dish surely will become one of your new favorites. When I had my first pork katsu cutlet, my mind was blown, and hopefully yours can be too!

Ingredients:

  • 2 slices Boneless pork loin or pork chop
  • 1 cup Panko bread crumbs
  • 1/2 cup all purpose flour
  • 1 Egg- beaten
  • Salt and pepper
  • Vegetable oil for frying

Instructions:

  1. Prep the pork loins: Cut slits into the white connective tissue on the outer edge of each pork loin or pork chop. This prevents it from curling up as it cooks. Flatten each pork loin to about ¾ inch thickness.
  2. Dredge the pork loins: Add the egg, flour, and panko bread crumbs to separate bowls. For a crispier crust, mix a tablespoon of flour with the egg to create a thicker egg wash for more bread crumbs to adhere to. Salt and pepper the pork, then coat it in flour and shake off the excess. Next, dip it into the beaten egg and then firmly press it into the panko bread crumbs for a thick, generous layer of breading. See Note 2 for a crispier crust.
  3. Heat Oil: In a large heavy-duty pot or fryer, add enough oil to deep fry the cutlets. Heat the oil to 340°F over medium heat to ensure the pork fully cooks without burning the bread crumbs.
  4. Fry the pork cutlets:Gently place a pork cutlet into the oil and deep-fry for 5 to 6 minutes per side or until the pork is fully cooked and the panko coating is golden brown and crispy.
  5. Serve: Slice the tonkatsu and serve with tonkatsu sauce, shredded cabbage, and rice. Enjoy!
